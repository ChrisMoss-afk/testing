Product rationale

Polytechnic Pulse is the campus layer between scattered information and student attention.

The product is designed to outlive one student team by making content easy to replace, governance visible, and future upgrades obvious.

.txt plain-text content files keep the prototype maintainable

Why it exists

Students should not need to search five places to understand one campus week.

Polytechnic Pulse centralizes timely updates while preserving the role of official RDP departments, SARDP, clubs, services, and community partners.

What it is not

It is not a replacement for RDP or SARDP.

The hub should route users toward the right source. It wins by organizing attention, not by pretending to own every source of truth.

Governance

Every update needs a standard.

Future operators should tag each item as verified campus update, student-submitted, partner-submitted, or paid placement. Trust is the product's real moat.

Employment board

Jobs belong here because student attention and local workforce demand already intersect.

The Work Board should not replace official career services. It should increase discovery, create sponsorable inventory, and help local employers reach students through a clearer campus-facing channel.

Next upgrade

Move from static prototype to CMS when the workflow is proven.

The text-file structure gives non-coders control now. Once the operating rhythm works, the same fields can migrate into Airtable, WordPress, Webflow CMS, Sanity, or another managed backend.
